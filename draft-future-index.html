Counseling Focused on the Whole Person

I am a Master's student in Clinical Mental Health Counseling at Merrimack College. My clinical practice is founded on a deep respect for the individual and the belief that everyone has an innate capacity for wholeness.

Effective therapy involves looking at how your brain and body work together to navigate the world. I integrate the Theory of Constructed Emotion with Coherence Therapy, parts work, and other evidence-based approaches to help you update unhelpful patterns and find freedom in your daily life.

Recognizing the Pattern

Your symptoms often carry a coherent message. You might see yourself in one of these common experiences:

Chronic Pain or Fatigue

You deal with low energy, pain, migraines, or tension that flares up intermittently. These physical symptoms often occur when you have reached the limit of what you can internalize.

Burnout & Procrastination

You find yourself stuck in cycles of perfectionism, procrastination, and burnout. This is a protective response from a system that feels overwhelmed by internal and external expectations.

Dissociation & Numbness

You often feel disconnected from your own life. This foggy or numb sensation is an understandable way for your mind to manage environments that feel overwhelming (perhaps due to trauma or chronic stress).

Feeling "Talked Out" in Therapy

You have spent years in therapy or you understand your story well. You feel hopeless because intellectual insight has not yet shifted the heavy emotional weight you carry.
