Is This You?

Recognizing the Pattern

You might recognize yourself in one of these descriptions:

Insight without change

You understand your patterns. You can trace the anxiety back to where it started. And yet it keeps coming back. You've left sessions thinking: "that was helpful, but..."

A sharp inner critic

You manage everything on the surface. You're the capable one. But underneath, something has been building, and the environment you're in rewards you for continuing to push it down.

Chronic pain or fatigue

You deal with low energy, pain, migraines, or tension that flares up without clear cause. These physical symptoms often arrive when you've reached the limit of what you can hold.

Disconnection and numbness

You feel distant from your own life. Foggy. Like you're watching it from the outside. This is an understandable response to environments that have felt chronically overwhelming.

Burnout and perfectionism

You cycle between striving and collapse. Perfectionism, procrastination, and exhaustion take turns. It feels like your system is doing its best under impossible conditions.

Grief that hasn't lifted

Something happened... you lost someone, a relationship ended, or a version of your life didn't work out, and it's still too heavy.

My Approach

Therapy that works

Therapy should do more than help you cope. I believe it can change the underlying emotional logic that has been running the show.

01

Mindful, Somatic Awareness

We pay attention to what your body is holding, not just what your mind can articulate. Sensations, images, and deeper feelings are often where the real information lives.

02

Parts Work

Drawing on various approaches to parts work (Gestalt, Internal Family Systems or IFS, etc.), we explore with curiosity rather than judgment. We try to understand the role each part plays in your life.

03

Memory Reconsolidation

Through the lens of coherence, we work to update the deep, unconscious beliefs your system formed long ago to keep you safe. What inner knowings are no longer serving you?
